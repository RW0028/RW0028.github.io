About

I am an undergraduate student at the University of California, Davis, pursuing a double major in Neurobiology, Physiology & Behavior and Music. I am on a neuron-centered neuroscience track, with primary academic interests in understanding neural systems at the neuronal and circuit levels and how neural activity gives rise to physiological function.

As a pre-med student, I approach neuroscience not only as an intellectual pursuit, but also as a discipline closely connected to human health and clinical reality. This perspective motivates my focus on mechanisms that bridge neural activity and physiological function, while maintaining a long-term interest in translational relevance.

Alongside my core academic training in neuroscience, I maintain a sustained engagement with music as a parallel discipline. This background informs how I think about human experience and expression, while my academic and research trajectory remains firmly grounded in neuroscience and neural systems.

Research

  • Current Research. Research assistant in a neuroscience laboratory, focusing on neuron-centered questions in neural systems and physiology, with emphasis on neuronal and circuit-level mechanisms.
  • Past Research Experience. Prior experience in computational and engineering-oriented neuroscience (dry lab), involving data analysis and modeling approaches. This work has concluded and helped clarify my preference for biologically grounded, neuron-focused research.
  • Early Research Experience. Research on neurodegenerative disease mechanisms, focusing on Alzheimer’s disease and the role of microglia in neural pathology. Topics included endoplasmic reticulum stress, oxidative stress, autophagic–lysosomal pathways, and neuroinflammation.
    “Different Roles of Microglia in Alzheimer’s Disease,” International Conference on Biological Engineering and Medical Science (ICBioMed), 2023.
